Tamale Recipe

Tamales are a cherished staple in Latin American cuisine, celebrated for their comforting, homemade taste and cultural significance. Made from soft, steamed corn dough known as masa, tamales are filled with a variety of savory ingredients such as spiced meats, cheeses, or vegetables. The fillings are expertly seasoned, offering a rich and flavorful contrast to the mild, slightly sweet masa. Wrapped in corn husks or banana leaves, which impart a subtle earthiness during the cooking process, tamales are steamed to perfection.

The result is a dish thatâ€™s both tender and hearty, with each bite revealing a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Often served during holidays and special gatherings, tamales bring people together, offering a taste of tradition and a connection to heritage.

Ingredients

  • Masa Harina - 4 cups (corn flour for tamales)
  • Chicken Broth - 3 or 4 cups (to moisten the masa)
  • Lard - 1 cup (for a rich, tender texture)
  • Baking Powder - 1 teaspoon (to help the masa rise slightly)
  • Salt - 1 teaspoon (for seasoning the masa)
  • Corn Husks - soaked, for wrapping
  • Filling - such as shredded chicken, pork, beef, cheese, or vegetables, seasoned with chili sauce, mole, or other spices
  • Chili Sauce - for seasoning the filling (optional)
  • Garlic and Onion - finely chopped, for additional flavor in the filling (optional)
